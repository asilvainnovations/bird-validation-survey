Why You're Here

What Your Input Actually Does

4 Reasons It Matters
01
You Check Our Assumptions
The BIRD development team, in collaboration with government agencies, development partners, and sector experts, has drafted a picture of BARMM's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats. You're being asked whether that picture actually matches what you see on the ground -- in your community, sector, or province.
02

Your ratings are combined with everyone else's to score how resilient, risky, or urgent each issue really is -- turning many individual opinions into a clear, shared priority list that government planners, private investors, and development partners can all use.

The roadmap involves P120-160 billion in investment across agriculture, industry, infrastructure, trade, and finance over the next decade. Your input helps the Bangsamoro government, private sector, and development partners decide the order and priority of that rollout.
04
Your Values Stay Front and Center
This roadmap is meant to reflect Islamic ethical principles, cultural authenticity, and shared community benefit -- not just economic targets. Your responses, alongside input from civil society and community leaders, help keep it grounded in what BARMM actually values.
The Spirit Behind the Survey

The C.A.R.E. Framework

4 Pillars · Khalifa-Aligned
C
Context-Specific
This survey was written for BARMM, not copied from somewhere else. Questions reflect real provincial realities -- Maguindanao's services economy, Lanao del Sur's agro-industrial corridor, Tawi-Tawi's seaweed farms -- so you're answering about the place you actually know.
A
Action-Oriented
Nothing here is asked just to fill space. Every question connects to a real decision -- where money goes, what gets built first, what policy gets prioritized. Your answers feed directly into those choices.
R
Real & Responsive
BARMM's economy doesn't move in a straight line -- sectors affect each other, and today's fix can create tomorrow's problem. This survey is designed to capture that complexity, not force it into a simple checklist.
E
Evidence-Based
Your answers are combined with verified government data, halal industry standards, academic research, and internationally recognized development benchmarks -- so your voice is weighed alongside solid evidence, not lost in guesswork.
How Your Ratings Are Used

What Your 1-5 Ratings Actually Mean

4 Types of Questions
Questions About Strengths
  • You'll rate how strong something is, and how confident you are in that
  • Higher ratings tell us "this is a real strength we can build on"
  • Example: BARMM's halal identity -- most people rate this very high
  • Helps identify what BARMM should lean into, not just fix
Questions About Opportunities
  • You'll rate how promising an opportunity is, and how likely it is to happen
  • These help rank which opportunities are worth pursuing first
  • Example: the ASEAN halal market -- a widely recognized opportunity
  • Your view helps separate realistic bets from wishful thinking
Questions About Weaknesses
  • You'll rate how serious a gap or problem is, and how often it comes up
  • Higher ratings flag the issues holding BARMM back the most
  • Example: infrastructure gaps -- commonly rated as a major constraint
  • Your honesty here directly shapes what gets prioritized for fixing
Questions About Threats
  • You'll rate how damaging a threat could be, and how likely it is
  • Severe, likely threats get flagged for closer attention and planning
  • Example: climate shocks like El Nino -- widely rated as high-impact
  • Helps the roadmap prepare for risks instead of being caught off guard
Your Privacy

Your Data, Protected

What Happens With Your Answers
Anonymized in Reporting
  • Your name and contact details are never published
  • Only aggregated, combined results appear in reports and dashboards
  • No individual response can be traced back to you publicly
  • You can answer honestly without fear of being personally identified
Handled Securely
  • Your response is transmitted and stored through secure, encrypted channels
  • Only authorized platform administrators can access raw submissions
  • Your device also keeps a private local copy so you never lose progress
  • If you're offline, your answers are saved and sent once you're back online
Used Only for This Roadmap
  • Your responses are used solely to validate and refine the BIRD 2026-2035 roadmap
  • Data isn't sold, shared with advertisers, or repurposed for unrelated use
  • Results are reviewed by the Bangsamoro government, development partners, and stakeholder advisory bodies to guide real decisions
  • You can request more detail on data handling from the BIRD development team at any time
Your Choice, Your Consent
  • Participation is voluntary -- you may skip the survey or stop at any time
  • You'll be asked to explicitly consent before your response is submitted
  • Optional questions are clearly marked; you're never forced to overshare
  • Providing your name and email is only for us to send you a copy of your answers

Common Questions

Questions Stakeholders Often Ask

6 Quick Answers
How Long?
"I'm busy -- how long will this really take?"
The survey is designed to take about 15-20 minutes for most respondents, depending on how much detail you choose to add in the open-text questions.
Good to know: Your progress is saved automatically as you go, so you can stop partway and pick up right where you left off later.
Not Sure?
"What if I'm not sure about an answer?"
You don't need to be an expert on every topic. Answer based on what you genuinely know or observe -- there are no wrong answers, only honest ones.
Good to know: Each question includes plain-language context, and some let you skip if a topic truly isn't relevant to you.
Connection?
"What if my internet cuts out midway?"
No problem. The survey keeps working and saving your answers locally on your device even without a connection.
Good to know: Once you're back online, your response is submitted automatically -- you don't need to start over.
Feels Long?
"There are 16 sections -- is that a lot?"
Each section is short and focused on one topic at a time, so it moves quickly even though there are many parts.
Good to know: A progress bar always shows exactly where you are, and you can jump back to review earlier answers.
Honesty?
"Is it safe to answer honestly about sensitive topics?"
Some questions touch on governance, security, or resource allocation. Your candid view is exactly what's needed here.
Good to know: Your answers are anonymized in all reporting -- no one can trace an individual response back to you.
Respect?
"Does this survey respect our culture and values?"
The survey was built around Khalifa stewardship principles and reviewed with cultural sensitivity toward BARMM's Islamic and indigenous traditions, in consultation with community leaders, religious scholars, and civil society representatives.
Good to know: If anything ever feels off, the BIRD development team welcomes that feedback -- it helps us improve future rounds of engagement.
